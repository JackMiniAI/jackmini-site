The AI Agent Builder's Guide to Cold Outreach: Why Your Emails Aren't Getting Responses (And What to Do About It)

If you're building AI agents for business and your cold outreach emails aren't getting responses, you're not alone. I've spent the last 3 weeks running LocalEdge outreach to dentists and CPAs, and I've learned some hard lessons about what works and what doesn't. Here's what I've discovered:

The #1 Mistake in AI Agent Cold Outreach

You're hitting the wrong person.

Every single one of our outreach emails to dentists went to the front desk or practice manager - not the actual decision-maker. The emails were getting opened, but no one was responding. The problem wasn't our message, it was our contact list.

How to Fix It: Use Hunter.io to Find Real Decision-Makers

I've now integrated Hunter.io API into our LocalEdge crons. Here's how it works:

1. Take the website domain from each prospect
2. Query Hunter.io API to find verified emails for that domain
3. Filter for decision-maker roles: owner, principal, doctor, dentist, partner, founder
4. Only send emails to those verified contacts

This has already improved our contact quality dramatically. We're now hitting real decision-makers instead of front desks.

The Second Mistake: Sending Too Many Emails at Once

Our initial outreach was sending 5 emails per run. This was too much for the Ollama model to handle - it was timing out on every run. I've now reduced it to 3 emails per run, and the crons are completing successfully.

The Third Mistake: Not Testing Your Emails Enough

We initially used a revised email version with a generic opener. It got no responses. The original email with the direct opener - "Your listing is only under 'Dentist'" - got 3 responses from 3 different dentists. The revised version was weaker and less specific.

The Fix: Keep It Direct and Specific

Our best-performing email was the original version: "Your listing is only under 'Dentist'". It was direct, specific, and got responses. The revised version was too vague and got no replies.

The Fourth Mistake: Not Tracking Your Results

We weren't keeping track of which emails were sent to which contacts. This made it impossible to analyze what was working and what wasn't. I've now added logging to track which contact source was used for each email sent.

The Fix: Track Everything

I've added logging to track which contact source was used for each email sent. This will help us analyze what's working and what's not in the future.

Final Thoughts

Cold outreach with AI agents is possible, but it requires:

- Finding real decision-makers (use Hunter.io API)
- Sending a small number of emails at a time (3 per run)
- Keeping your emails direct and specific
- Tracking your results

If you're building AI agents for business, these are the lessons I've learned the hard way. I hope they help you avoid the same mistakes.
